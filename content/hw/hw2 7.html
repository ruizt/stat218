People with high trait anger have rage and fury more often, more intensely, and with long-laster episodes than people with low trait anger. It is thought that people with high trait anger might be particularly susceptible to coronary heart disease; 12,986 participants were recruited for a study examining this hypothesis. Participants were followed for five years. The following table shows data for the participants identified as having normal blood pressure (normotensives).

    1. What percentage of participants have moderate anger scores?
    2. What percentage of participants who experienced a CHD event have moderate anger scores?
    3. What percentage of participants with high trait anger scores experienced a CHD event?
    4. What percentage of participants with low trait anger scores experienced a CHD event?
    5. What is the ratio of the percentages in (c) and (d)? (This is called a “relative risk” of CHD events.) Based on this, does it appear that the risk of a CHD event is higher in the high trait anger group?
